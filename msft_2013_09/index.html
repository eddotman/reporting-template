Monthly Status Report


September 2013

Overview

Startup Canada has been working in partnership with Microsoft to support entrepreneurs in Canada through a variety of programming including Facebook posts, #startupchats hosted on Twitter, events and articles featured in PIVOT magazine.

As a founding Platinum sponsor, Microsoft can expect the following commitments from Startup Canada:

  • Identify Microsoft Canada as a Founding Platinum sponsor of all its entrepreneurship programming.
  • Recognize Microsoft Canada in all press releases (prior consent required from Microsoft in all cases).
  • Display visible Microsoft Canada banner stands at all events.
  • Provide Microsoft with podium opportunities, VIP invites and government relations opportunities.

Highlights

#StartupChats

On 11 September 2013, Startup Canada hosted a #StartupChats session with Marc Gagne of Microsoft on the topic of organizational culture. The session had 15 participants, 5 listeners, 16 retweets and 10 favourites. Through Twitter, Startup Canada was able to reach a network of over 919 000 followers. A summary of the chat can be seen on Storify. The official description of the #StartupChats session is provided below:

An organization's immune system, a code of civility, the glue that binds employees, organizational culture can be endlessly described. Most will agree that it plays a key part in shaping a workplace & subsequently a brand, but the road to amazing organizational culture looks different to each of us. Listen & join in to a chat with Marc Gagne for tips on developing the right culture to suit your Startup's needs & brand image.
